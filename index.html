🥢 Soy Honey Sesame Dressing
Salmon days ▼

A light, punchy dressing for the shredded cabbage in your salmon bowl. Make a 4-serving batch on meal prep day and store in a jar — lasts a week in the fridge.

Ingredients — 1 serving
Soy sauce (low sodium) — 1 tbsp
Rice vinegar — 1 tbsp
Sesame oil — 1 tsp
Hot honey — 1 tsp
Garlic powder — pinch
Steps
1
Add all ingredients to a small bowl or jar.
2
Whisk together or seal the jar and shake well until fully combined.
3
Dress the cabbage fresh right before eating — don't pre-dress or it goes soggy.
💡 Batch tip: multiply everything by 4 for a week's worth. Sesame oil will separate in the fridge — just shake before using.
🐟 White Miso Salmon Marinade
Salmon days ▼

A simple miso glaze that caramelizes beautifully in the oven. White miso is mild and slightly sweet — it pairs perfectly with the hot honey and gives your salmon a restaurant-quality finish without adding much to your macros.

Ingredients — 1 fillet (113g)
White miso paste — 1 tbsp (~18g)
Hot honey — 1 tsp
Soy sauce (low sodium) — 1 tsp
Rice vinegar — ½ tsp
Garlic powder — pinch
Steps
1
Mix all marinade ingredients together in a small bowl until smooth.
2
Pat salmon fillet dry with a paper towel — this helps the marinade stick and the skin crisp up.
3
Spread marinade evenly over the top of the fillet. For best results, marinate for 20–30 min in the fridge (or even overnight).
4
Bake at 400°F for 10–12 min. For meal prep, pull at 125–130°F internal so it doesn't dry out when reheated.
5
Optional: broil for the last 1–2 min to caramelize the miso glaze on top.
💡 Meal prep: marinate all 4 fillets together in a container overnight, then bake Sunday evening. The miso actually gets better the longer it sits.
